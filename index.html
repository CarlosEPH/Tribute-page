Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

It is only with the heart that one can see rightly; what is essential is invisible to the eye.
Antoine Marie Jean-Baptiste Roger Conde de Saint-Exupéry
Was a French writer, poet, aristocrat, journalist and pioneering aviator. He became a laureate of several of France's highest literary awards and also won the United States National Book Award. He is best remembered for his novella The Little Prince (Le Petit Prince) and for his lyrical aviation writings, including Wind, Sand and Stars and Night Flight. More info.

TIME LINE

JUL 31, 1900

Antoine de Saint Exupery was born.

OCT 2, 1904

His father tragically died from a stroke. Which was 4 years after he was born. The death of his father greatly impacted his family's life.

OCT 2, 1915

He attended Jesuit schools in Montgre and Le Mans, and a catholic Boarding school in Switzerland.

OCT 2, 1921

He began his military service with the 2nd Regiment of Light Calvary. And was sent to Neuhof, near Stratsburg. When he was there he took private flying lessons and the following year was offered a transfer from the French Army to the French Air Force.

OCT 2, 1926

He was flying again. I'm he was very anxious to start again. He became one of the pioneers of international postal flight.

OCT 2, 1931

He married Consuelo Suncin. She was a twice-widowed Salvadoran Countess, writer, and artist.

DEC 30, 1935

He crashed in the Sahara desert. He was trying to break a record with Andre Prevot and win 150,000 franc. The aircraft was a Coudron Simnoun, and the crash site was near the Wadi Natrun Valley.

OCT 2, 1942

He wrote The Little Prince. Which is the most translated novel in the world. He worked on the Little Prince for the whole summer and part of autumn.

OCT 2, 1943

He departed to fly with the Free French Air Force and flight with the Allies in a Mediteranean-based squad.

JUL 31, 1944

He died.
